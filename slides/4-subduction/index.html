Overview

  • Subduction is a big part of global tectonics
  • Part of convenction in the mantle
  • Important global cycles: water and carbon
  • Understanding subduction is key to understanding the deep Earth

Image Subduction-en.svg from Wikimedia Commons by K. D. Schroeder, CC-BY-SA 4.0

Why does subduction happen?

Models predict that plates cool away from the ridge

  • As the plate cools, it gets denser
  • When the density is greater than that of the asthenosphere:
    • Gravitational instability
    • Plate sinks into asthenosphere
  • Sinking slab pulls the plate behind it (slab pull).

Imaging subducting slabs

  • Subducting slab heats and deforms causing deep earthquakes (70-300km)
  • This region is known as the Wadati-Benioff zone
  • Surrounding asthenosphere ductile and cannot cause earthquakes
  • Hypocenters give an image of the slab
  • Still used to determine slab geometry*

* For example, in the Slab2 model.

At the surface

Example from the Mariana Trench subduction.

  • Subducting plate bends under the load, creating trenches
  • Bending causes a forebulge next to the trench
  • Island arcs are often associated with the overlying plate

* In the example, the Pacific plate is older (hence cooler and denser). How can we tell from comparing the bathymetry the two plates?

At the subsurface

Image Cross_section_of_mariana_trench.svg from Wikimedia Commons by V. Ezekowitz, CC-BY-SA 3.0

  • Subduction brings water (in minerals) to the mantle
  • Hydration reduces the melting point of mantle rocks
  • Melt rises to the surface
  • Associated volcanism creates island arcs

Flexure of the lithosphere

Flexure of an elastic plate at equilibrium:

$ D\dfrac{\partial^4 w}{\partial x^4} = q(x) - (\rho_m - \rho_c) g w $ $D = \dfrac{E T_e^2}{12(1 - \nu^2)}$

$w$ = deflection, $q(x)$ = force density of load, $g$ = gravity,
$D$ = flexural rigidity, $E$ = Young's modulus,
$\nu$ = Poisson's ratio, $T_e$ = effective elastic thickness

Boundary conditions

Flexure under an end load:

  • No flexure away from the trench: $w \to 0$ as $x \to \infty$
  • Load $-V_0$ is applied at $x = 0$
  • The bending moment at $x= 0$ is $M_0$

Solution

  • Problem: In reality $M_0$ and $V_0$ are unknown.
  • Write solution as a function of $w_b$, $x_0$, and $x_b$.

$ \dfrac{w}{w_b}(x) = e^{\pi/4} \sqrt{2} \exp\left[ -\dfrac{\pi}{4}\left(\dfrac{x - x_0}{x_b - x_0}\right) \right] \sin\left[\dfrac{\pi}{4}\left(\dfrac{x - x_0}{x_b - x_0}\right) \right] $
