Side note

Coordinate systems and map projections

Geodetic coordinates

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Geodetic_coordinates.svg (CC-BY-SA 3.0)

  • $O$ = Earth's center of mass
  • $\phi$ = Latitude
  • $\lambda$ = Longitude
  • $h$ = geometric (ellipsoidal) height

Geocentric coordinates

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Geocentric_coords_02.svg (CC-BY-SA 4.0)

  • $O$ = Earth's center of mass
  • $\theta$ = Latitude (geocentric)
  • $\theta'$ = Colatitude
  • $\lambda$ = Longitude (same)
  • $r$ = geocentric radius

Local North Oriented coordinates

Source: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:ECEF_ENU_Longitude_Latitude_relationships.svg

  • $O$ = Point on surface
  • $(X, Y, Z)_{ecef}$ = Geocentric Cartesian
  • $(x, y, z)_{lno}$ = Topocentric Cartesian

Map projections

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Comparison_of_cartography_surface_development.svg (CC-BY-SA 4.0)

  • Geodetic to Cartesian
  • Need to represent data in maps accurately
  • Distortions always happen
  • Choice depends on use case
  • Examples: Mercator, UTM, Robinson
